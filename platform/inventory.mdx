Running out of stock is never a good look. Qorelly's inventory tools help you stay on top of what you have, know when to reorder, and keep a clear record of every change.

## At a glance

When you open the Inventory section, the dashboard shows you the big picture:

- How many items you're tracking
- Which items are running low
- The total value of your stock
- Recent stock changes

## Your stock items

Go to **Inventory > Stock** to see everything you're tracking.

For each item, you can see:

| | What it tells you |
|-------|-------------|
| **Name** | The item (linked to your menu) |
| **Quantity** | How many you have right now |
| **Unit** | How it's measured (pieces, kg, litres, etc.) |
| **Reorder point** | The number where you should restock |
| **Unit cost** | What each one costs you |
| **Last restocked** | When it was last topped up and by who |

### Low stock alerts

When any item drops below its reorder point, Qorelly flags it for you. You'll see these alerts on the inventory dashboard and in the POS alerts section — so you never get caught off guard.

## Suppliers

Go to **Inventory > Suppliers** to keep all your supplier information in one place.

For each supplier you can save:

- Their company name and contact person
- Phone, email, and address
- Payment terms (like "net 30" or "pay on delivery")
- Credit limit
- Whether they're currently active

## Purchase orders

When it's time to restock, go to **Inventory > Purchase Orders** to place an order with your supplier.

### Creating a purchase order

1. Click **New Purchase Order**
2. Choose which supplier you're ordering from
3. Add the items you need, with quantities and prices
4. Submit it

### Tracking your order

| Status | What it means |
|--------|--------------|
| Draft | You've started the order but haven't sent it yet |
| Ordered | It's been sent to the supplier |
| Partial | Some items have arrived, but not all |
| Received | Everything's in — stock levels are updated |

You can also track whether you've paid for the order: pending, partially paid, or fully paid.

When you mark items as received, your stock quantities update right away.

## Stock history

Go to **Inventory > Movements** to see a complete history of every stock change. Every entry records:

- **What changed** — Which item and how much
- **Why** — Was it a purchase, a sale, an adjustment, waste, or a return?
- **Before and after** — The stock level before and after the change
- **Who did it** — Which team member made the change
- **When** — The exact date and time

This gives you a full trail you can look back at anytime.

## Manual adjustments

Sometimes you need to correct your numbers — maybe after a stock count, or to write off damaged goods. Go to **Inventory > Adjustments** to make manual corrections.

Every adjustment is recorded in your stock history, so there's always a clear record of what changed and why.
