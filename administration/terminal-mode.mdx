---
title: "Terminal Mode"
description: "Shared device access with PIN-based staff login"
---

Terminal Mode is designed for shared devices like a POS terminal at a counter or a tablet on the shop floor. Instead of each staff member logging in with their email and password, they use a short PIN code.

## How it works

1. An owner or manager sets up a device in Terminal Mode
2. The device stays logged in to the business account
3. Individual staff members clock in using their PIN
4. All actions are attributed to the PIN-authenticated staff member
5. When done, the staff member locks the terminal

## Setting up Terminal Mode

### On the web

1. Navigate to `/auth/terminal-login`
2. The device enters Terminal Mode and displays a PIN entry screen
3. Staff members enter their PIN to access the system

### Assigning PINs

Each team member needs a PIN to use Terminal Mode:

1. Go to **Team**
2. Edit a team member
3. Set their **PIN code** (typically 4-6 digits)
4. Save

<Warning>
  PINs should be unique per team member within your business. Avoid simple sequences like 1234 or 0000.
</Warning>

## What staff can access

In Terminal Mode, access is simplified to the most common operational tasks:

- **POS** — Create and manage orders
- **Bookings** — View and manage reservations
- **Order history** — Review recent orders

Administrative features like settings, team management, and reports are not accessible in Terminal Mode.

## Terminal lock screen

When no staff member is actively using the terminal, a lock screen is displayed. To unlock:

1. The staff member enters their PIN
2. The system verifies the PIN against team member records
3. On success, the terminal opens with the staff member's session
4. All subsequent actions are logged under that staff member

## Staff session tracking

Terminal Mode tracks:

- Which staff member is currently active
- When they clocked in
- All orders and actions during their session

This data feeds into the team activity log for accountability and reporting.

## Exiting Terminal Mode

To exit Terminal Mode and return to normal login:

1. An owner or manager can disable Terminal Mode from the device
2. The device returns to the standard login screen

## Use cases

- **Restaurant counter** — A shared tablet where waiters and cashiers take turns
- **Retail checkout** — A POS terminal used by multiple cashiers across shifts
- **Salon front desk** — A shared device where receptionists manage bookings
