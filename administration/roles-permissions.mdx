Qorelly uses role-based access control (RBAC) to determine what each team member can see and do within the platform. Every action is checked against the user's assigned role and its permissions.

## Built-in roles

| Role | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Owner | The business creator. Full access to all features, settings, and billing. Cannot be removed. |
| Manager | Full operational access. Can manage team, orders, inventory, and most settings. |
| Cashier | POS-focused. Can create and process orders, handle payments. |
| Waiter | Service-focused. Can view orders, create new orders, and manage table assignments. |
| Chef | Kitchen-focused. Can view orders and update preparation status. |

<Info>
  Role names vary by [business type](/business-types). Spas have "Therapist" and "Stylist", pharmacies have "Pharmacist", etc.
</Info>

## Permission modules

Permissions are organized by module, with specific actions for each:

### Dashboard
| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|

## Custom roles

Create roles with exactly the permissions your team needs:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Team">
    Go to **Team** and find the **Roles** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a new role">
    Click **Create Role** and give it a name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set permissions">
    Toggle individual permissions for each module. Select only the actions this role needs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign the role">
    When inviting or editing team members, select your custom role from the role dropdown.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How permissions are enforced

Permissions are enforced at two levels:

1. **Navigation** — Sidebar items and pages are hidden if the user lacks the `view` permission for that module
2. **Actions** — Buttons and features (like "New Order" or "Delete") are hidden or disabled based on specific action permissions

On the web platform, a `ProtectedRoute` component checks permissions before rendering each page. On mobile, the app mode and role work together to control what's visible.

## Route protection example

When a team member navigates to a page they don't have access to, they are redirected to the dashboard. There is no error message — the restricted pages simply don't appear in their navigation.

## Best practices

- **Principle of least privilege** — Give each role only the permissions it needs
- **Use built-in roles first** — They cover the most common patterns. Create custom roles only when needed.
- **Review regularly** — Audit team member roles periodically to ensure access levels are still appropriate
- **Test with a new member** — After creating a custom role, log in as a team member with that role to verify the experience
