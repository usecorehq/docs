Qorelly supports multiple business types, each with tailored terminology, default modules, and UI configurations. You select your business type during setup, and you can change it later in **Settings > Business**.

## Supported business types

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Restaurant" icon="utensils">
    The default business type. Includes full support for:
    - **Menu** with dishes organized by categories
    - **POS** with dine-in, takeaway, and delivery order types
    - **Tables** and **floor plan** management
    - **Bookings** for reservations
    - **Kitchen workflow** — orders move through Pending, Preparing, Ready, Completed

    **Terminology:** Menu items are called "dishes", staff includes "chefs" and "waiters", locations are "branches".
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Spa / Salon" icon="spa">
    Optimized for appointment-based service businesses:
    - **Services** replace menu items
    - **Bookings** is the primary module with time-slot scheduling
    - **Rooms** for service delivery
    - **Staff** includes therapists and stylists

    **Terminology:** Menu items are called "services", order status uses "being worked on" instead of "preparing", staff roles include "therapist" and "stylist".
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Pharmacy" icon="pills">
    Tailored for pharmaceutical retail:
    - **Products** replace menu items with SKU and barcode support
    - **Inventory** tracking with reorder points and supplier management
    - **POS** for over-the-counter sales
    - **Customer** profiles for purchase history

    **Terminology:** Menu items are called "medicines", order status uses "processing", staff includes "pharmacists".
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Retail" icon="store">
    For general retail and e-commerce:
    - **Products** with SKU, barcode, and variant support
    - **Inventory** management with suppliers and purchase orders
    - **POS** with barcode scanning
    - **Customer** management and loyalty

    **Terminology:** Menu items are called "products", order status uses "packing", locations are "stores".
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Hospitality" icon="hotel">
    For hotels, lodges, and guest houses:
    - **Rooms** management
    - **Bookings** for reservations
    - **POS** for in-house services
    - **Customer** guest profiles
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Fuel Station" icon="gas-pump">
    For fuel stations and convenience stores:
    - **POS** for fuel and shop sales
    - **Inventory** for fuel and product tracking
    - **Shift management** with work periods
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## How business type affects the platform

| Feature | Restaurant | Spa/Salon | Pharmacy | Retail |
|---------|-----------|----------|----------|--------|
| Menu item label | Dish | Service | Medicine | Product |
| Order status (in progress) | Preparing | Being worked on | Processing | Packing |
| Primary staff roles | Chef, Waiter | Therapist, Stylist | Pharmacist | Manager |
| Location label | Branch | Outlet | Store | Location |
| Default modules | Menu, POS, Bookings | Bookings, Menu | POS, Inventory | POS, Inventory |

## Changing your business type

1. Go to **Settings > Business**
2. Select your new business type
3. The platform will update all terminology and default configurations

<Warning>
  Changing your business type updates labels and defaults across the platform. Your data (menu items, orders, customers) is preserved, but the way it is displayed will change.
</Warning>
